Comms · Page Specification

Comms Home

The landing point for the Comms top-level nav item — recent calls, texts, and chat rooms in one place, and the entry point for starting any of the three.

Purpose

Three independently-gated sections sharing one screen: view_calls controls the calls section, view_sms controls the texts section, and chat room visibility is invite-based (or view_all_chats). None of these three overlap with place_calls, which gates only initiating a call or SMS — a marina can have staff who see call/text history without being able to place calls, or who can place calls but can't browse past ones, per Permissions.

States & edge cases

  • No view_calls — the calls section (and its tab, on mobile) is omitted entirely, not shown empty; starting a new call is still possible with place_calls even without it, since the two are independent.
  • No view_sms — same treatment for the texts section.
  • No place_calls — the start-call and start-text actions are hidden, but existing history stays visible to anyone holding view_calls/view_sms; a marina can have staff who need call/text context without being able to place calls themselves.
  • Empty sections (as opposed to gated-off ones) — each has its own empty state rather than hiding the whole section.
  • Not invited to any chat room and no view_all_chats — the chat section shows only the "start a room" action with nothing listed, rather than being hidden outright.

Mobile vs. desktop

Mobile: calls, texts, and chat rooms as three switchable tabs to keep each list full-width and scannable. Desktop: the three sections can sit side by side in columns, since there's room to scan all of them without switching.
