Chat Room

One internal, topic-based conversation — entirely separate from calls and SMS, with no database relationship linking the two.

Purpose

Reached from Comms Home. Per Role Workflows — Comms: chat rooms, any user can start a room and invite specific users and/or roles into it; this page is where that room's conversation actually happens. Two distinct permissions govern access beyond actual participation: view_all_chats (read any room, participant or not) and manage_chats (add yourself or someone else as an actual participant to any room) — see Permissions.

States & edge cases

  • Not invited, no view_all_chats — the room isn't reachable or listed anywhere; there's no "request access" path.
  • view_all_chats holder viewing a room they aren't a participant of — full read access to the message history, but the composer (text box) is disabled. If they also hold manage_chats, a link overlays the disabled composer offering to join the conversation — tapping it performs the Join action above. Without manage_chats, the composer is simply disabled with no overlay; they can read, not act.
  • Offline — chat is ordinary InstantDB data, not a Twilio action, so it works fully offline (send, read, attach) and syncs on reconnect, unlike SMS.
  • Role membership changes mid-room — a user removed from an invited_roles role loses access to the room going forward, but past messages they sent remain (their authorship doesn't get retracted).
  • Joining, then leaving — no explicit "leave" action is modeled; a participant added via Join (or original invite) stays a participant unless a room creator or manage_chats holder removes them.

Mobile vs. desktop

Mobile: full-screen single room. Desktop: room list and active room can sit side by side, matching typical chat-app conventions.
